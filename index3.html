National Academies (1980)

Thompson was inducted into the National Academy of Engineering in 1980 for "developing UNIX, an operating system whose efficiency, breadth, power, and style have led a generation's minicomputer exploitation." He was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1985. (NAS).

Turning Award (1983)

The Turing Award was given to Thompson and Ritchie in 1983 "for their invention of general operating systems theory and especially for the development of the UNIX operating system." The backdoor assault commonly known as the Thompson hack or trusting trust attack was given in his acceptance speech, "Reflections on Trusting Trust," which is widely regarded as a fundamental computer security work in its own right.

IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal (1990)

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) awarded Thompson and Dennis Ritchie the IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal in 1990 "for the invention of the UNIX operating system and the C programming language."

Fellow of the Computer History Museum (1997)

Thompson and Ritchie were both named Fellows of the Computer History Museum in 1997 for "co-creating the UNIX operating system and developing the C programming language."

National Medal of Technology (1999)

On April 27, 1999, President Bill Clinton coinvented the UNIX operating system and C programming language, which together 'leaded to enormous progress in computer hardware, software and networking systems' and stimulated growth in the industry to boost American leadership in information, Thompson and Ritchie jointly received the 1998 National Medal of Technology

Tsutomu Kanai Award (1999)

In 1999, Thompson was awarded the first Tsutomu Kanai Award by the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers for "the role that he had played in building a UNIX functionality which had been a main platform for distributed systems development for decades."

Japan Prize (2011)

In 2011 Thompson was given the Japan Information and Communications Prize, together with Dennis Ritchie, for his pioneering contribution in developing the Unix operating system.
